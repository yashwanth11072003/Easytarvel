Places to visit

Kashi, also known as Varanasi, is one of the oldest and holiest cities in India, situated on the banks of the Ganges River in the state of Uttar Pradesh. It is a major pilgrimage site for Hindus and is known for its spiritual significance. If you're interested in exploring nearby places from Kashi, here are some suggestions:
1. Sarnath: Located just a few kilometers from Varanasi, Sarnath is an important Buddhist pilgrimage site. It is where Lord Buddha delivered his first sermon after attaining enlightenment. The Dhamek Stupa and the Ashoka Pillar are notable attractions in Sarnath.
2. Ramnagar Fort: Situated on the eastern bank of the Ganges River in Varanasi, Ramnagar Fort is a historical fort and palace. It houses a museum displaying vintage cars, royal costumes, and other artifacts.
3. Assi Ghat: Along the banks of the Ganges in Varanasi, Assi Ghat is a popular and ancient ghat known for its cultural and spiritual significance. It's a great place to experience the Ganga Aarti.
4. Kashi Vishwanath Temple: This temple is one of the most sacred and famous shrines dedicated to Lord Shiva. It attracts millions of pilgrims and devotees every year.
5. Manikarnika Ghat: One of the principal cremation ghats in Varanasi, Manikarnika Ghat is a place where Hindus believe cremation leads to moksha (liberation from the cycle of rebirth).
6. Bharat Kala Bhavan Museum: Located within the campus of Banaras Hindu University (BHU), this museum houses an extensive collection of art, artifacts, and archaeological objects.
7. Chunar Fort: Situated about 40 kilometers from Varanasi, Chunar Fort is a historic fort on the banks of the Ganges. It has witnessed various rulers and events throughout history.
