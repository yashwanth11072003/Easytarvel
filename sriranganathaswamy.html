Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple

Places to visit

The Sri Ranganatha Swamy Temple, also known as the Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple, is a major Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Ranganatha, a form of Lord Vishnu. It is located on an island in the Cauvery River in Srirangam, Tiruchirapalli (Trichy), Tamil Nadu, India. If you're interested in exploring nearby places from the Sri Ranganatha Swamy Temple, here are some suggestions:
1. Jambukeswarar Temple (Thiruvanaikaval): Located in the vicinity of Srirangam, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is one of the Pancha Bhoota Sthalas representing the element of water.
2. Sri Ayyappan Temple: Situated in Srirangam, this temple is dedicated to Lord Ayyappan. It is a popular pilgrimage site, especially during the annual Sabarimala season.
3. Rockfort Ucchi Pillayar Temple: Perched on a rock, this temple dedicated to Lord Ganesha offers panoramic views of Trichy. It is part of the Rockfort complex, which also includes the Thayumanaswami Temple.
4. Thayumanaswami Temple: Located at the base of the Rockfort, this temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It's known for its large Lingam and is associated with the legend of a devotee offering water to Shiva.
5. Sri Jambukeshwara Akilandeswari Temple: A unique temple dedicated to Goddess Parvati in the form of Akilandeswari, it is located in Thiruvanaikaval, near Srirangam.
6. Vekkaliamman Temple: Situated on the banks of the Cauvery River, this temple is dedicated to Goddess Vekkaliamman. It is known for its annual chariot festival.
7. Kallanai Dam: About 20 kilometers from Srirangam, the Kallanai Dam, also known as the Grand Anicut, is an ancient dam built across the Cauvery River. It is considered one of the oldest water-regulation structures in the world.
