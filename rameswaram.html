Rameswaram Temple

Places to visit

Rameswaram is a sacred town located on Pamban Island in the state of Tamil Nadu, India. It is known for its religious significance and is a popular pilgrimage destination. If you're interested in exploring nearby places from Rameswaram, here are some suggestions:
1. Ramanathaswamy Temple: This is the main attraction in Rameswaram, dedicated to Lord Shiva. The temple is famous for its long corridors and massive sculptured pillars. The Agni Theertham, a sacred sea bathing spot, is also associated with the temple.
2. Dhanushkodi: Located about 20 kilometers from Rameswaram, Dhanushkodi is a ghost town that was destroyed during a cyclone in 1964. The place is known for its serene beaches and the confluence of the Bay of Bengal and the Indian Ocean.
3. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam Memorial: Dedicated to India's former President and renowned scientist, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, this memorial is located in Pei Karumbu, around 2.5 kilometers from Rameswaram.
4. Gandhamadhana Parvatham: A hillock located on the island, Gandhamadhana Parvatham offers panoramic views of Rameswaram. It is believed to be the place where Lord Rama's feet are imprinted on a chakra.
5. Agniteertham: It is a sacred beach in Rameswaram where pilgrims take a dip before entering the Ramanathaswamy Temple. It is believed that the water has healing properties.
6. Five-Faced Hanuman Temple: This temple is dedicated to Lord Hanuman and is located near the Ramanathaswamy Temple. It is said to be the place where Hanuman revealed his five-faced form.
7. Kothandaramaswamy Temple: Situated on Dhanushkodi Beach, this temple is associated with the Ramayana and is believed to be the spot where Lord Rama performed the last rites for Jatayu, the mythical bird.
