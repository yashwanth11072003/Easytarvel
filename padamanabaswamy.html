Padamanaba Swamy Temple

Places to visit

The Padmanabhaswamy Temple is a famous Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, particularly in the form of Anantha Padmanabha (Vishnu lying on the cosmic serpent Ananta). It is located in Thiruvananthapuram, the capital city of Kerala, India. If you're interested in exploring nearby places from the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, here are some suggestions:
1. Napier Museum: Situated near the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, the Napier Museum is an art and natural history museum known for its architectural style and diverse collections, including bronze idols, ancient ornaments, and more.
2. Kuthira Malika (Puthen Malika) Palace: This historic palace, located near the Padmanabhaswamy Temple, is known for its unique architecture and is named "Kuthira Malika" or "Horse Palace." It was built by Maharaja Swathi Thirunal Balarama Varma.
3. Sree Chitra Art Gallery: Adjacent to the Napier Museum, the Sree Chitra Art Gallery displays a rich collection of paintings, sculptures, and artifacts. It's a great place for art enthusiasts.
4. Kowdiar Palace: While not open to the public, the Kowdiar Palace is the official residence of the Travancore royal family. The palace and its surroundings are known for their architectural beauty.
5. Attukal Bhagavathy Temple: Located a bit away from the city center, the Attukal Bhagavathy Temple is known for hosting the annual Attukal Pongala, which holds the Guinness World Record as the largest annual gathering of women.
6. Shangumugham Beach: If you prefer a relaxing time by the sea, Shangumugham Beach is a popular beach in Thiruvananthapuram. It's a great place for an evening stroll.
7. Veli Tourist Village: This village, situated near the Veli Lake, offers a serene environment with a floating bridge and well-maintained gardens. It's a family-friendly destination.
